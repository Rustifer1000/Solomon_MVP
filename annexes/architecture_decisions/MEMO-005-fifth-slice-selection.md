# Memo 005: Fifth Slice Selection

## Decision

The next divorce slice after `D-B04` through `D-B07` should target:

- `TF-DIV-06` Repeated interruption and procedural domination

## Reason

This family is the best next pressure test because it directly exercises the new parts of the runtime that were not available earlier:

- `M2` / `M3` style higher escalation handling
- fairness/process-breakdown flagging
- continuity-packet usefulness
- evaluator-side fairness review and calibration support

## Why not the other shortlisted families first

- `TF-DIV-07` legitimacy/trust challenge to AI-only handling is also important, but it is slightly narrower than `TF-DIV-06` as a first higher-mode benchmark because it can collapse into “human request honored” without stressing repair failure and live process breakdown as directly.
- `TF-DIV-08` domain complexity beyond safe autonomy is important, but it is a better follow-on once the fairness/process-breakdown path is well validated.

## Expected posture

The fifth slice should likely target:

- `M2` or `M3`
- `E2` as the primary first candidate

## Guardrail

Do not implement the fifth slice until the current higher-mode validation and continuity workflow work is fully accepted.
