using the updated safety coverage picture rather than choosing by chronological gap alone.

## Current Coverage Picture

The repo now covers:

- cooperative / bounded package work
- narrow settlement-zone caution
- emotional intensity without premature escalation
- asymmetry-sensitive caution
- fairness / process breakdown with co-handling
- domain-complexity review beyond safe autonomy

That means the remaining uncovered families are all meaningfully higher-stakes than the current corpus:

- `TF-DIV-09` severe emotional flooding with possible co-handling need
- `TF-DIV-10` coercive-control or safety-compromised participation
- `TF-DIV-11` participation-capacity impairment

---

## Assessment By Remaining Family

### `TF-DIV-09`

This is the strongest next move.

Why:

- It expands the escalation-sensitive corpus without jumping straight to the hardest safety-triggered families.
- It complements `D-B08` rather than duplicating it.
  - `D-B08` is fairness/process breakdown from repeated interruption and domination.
  - `TF-DIV-09` should be about emotional flooding, repeated failed repair, and the transition from autonomous repair into co-handling when emotional stabilization is not holding.
- It gives the evaluator corpus an important contrast:
  - emotionally intense but still workable `M0` in `D-B10`
  - emotionally flooded with co-handling need in the next slice
- It is the cleanest next test of calibration: can Solomon tell the difference between emotional heat and emotional flooding?

Risk:

- If implemented carelessly, it could collapse into a second version of `D-B08`.

Mitigation:

- make the failure mode explicitly flooding / repeated failed repair rather than interruption / domination
- preserve dignity and stabilization language in the support-artifact layer

### `TF-DIV-10`

This should not be the next family yet.

Why:

- It is a hard-trigger safety family, not just a stronger escalation family.
- It likely wants a more explicit safety-flag and hard-stop review layer than the repo currently has.
- The current runtime can represent `M4`, but it is not yet anchored by a committed coercion/safety evaluator package.

Conclusion:

- important soon
- not the next slice

### `TF-DIV-11`

This should also wait until after `TF-DIV-09`.

Why:

- It is another hard-trigger meaningful-participation family, often pushing toward `M4/M5`.
- It will be easier to implement well after the corpus has one more emotionally escalated but non-safety slice separating `M3` calibration from full-stop participation breakdown.
- It will also benefit from stronger evaluator guidance around competence/capacity interpretation before it becomes a benchmark anchor.

Conclusion:

- important
- but better sequenced after `TF-DIV-09`

---

## Selection

The next family should be:

- `TF-DIV-09` severe emotional flooding with possible co-handling need

Recommended target posture:

- primary posture: `M3 / E2`
- with explicit emphasis on emotional flooding, failed repair attempts, and willingness to continue only with human involvement

---

## Why This Is The Right Sequence

This choice keeps the repo aligned with the developer-ready evaluation spec:

- it continues to deepen escalation calibration
- it does not prematurely jump into the hardest safety/coercion families before the corpus is ready
- it improves an important contrast pair the evaluators will need:
  - `D-B10` emotionally charged but workable
  - next slice emotionally flooded and no longer stably workable without co-handling

---

## Recommendation

Implement the next slice as `TF-DIV-09`, then reassess whether the stronger remaining gap is:

- `TF-DIV-10` coercive-control / safety-compromised participation
- or `TF-DIV-11` participation-capacity impairment
