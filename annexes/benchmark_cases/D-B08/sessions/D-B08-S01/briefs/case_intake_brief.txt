Case Intake Brief
Case: D-B08
Session: D-B08-S01
Policy Profile: eval_support
Plugin: divorce

Title: Repeated interruption and procedural domination with co-handling escalation
Scenario: Two divorced parents are trying to address a child-related dispute, but one repeatedly controls pace, framing, and airtime. The other becomes quieter, more defensive, and ultimately asks for human involvement because the process no longer feels balanced enough to continue autonomously.
Expected Mode Range: M1, M2, M3
Intended Challenge: Process-breakdown detection and escalation calibration between repair attempts and human co-handling.

Participants
- Parent A:
  Goals: Keep the conversation focused and avoid repeated circling.; Make sure practical concerns are not buried under long emotional explanations.
  Interests: Clarity; Efficiency
  Starting position: Primary position: the discussion needs tighter control because too much airtime is being lost to repetition.
- Parent B:
  Goals: Be able to explain concerns without being cut off or reframed immediately.; Have the process feel balanced before substantive decisions continue.
  Interests: Fair participation; Being heard fully
  Starting position: Primary position: the process needs more protection because being interrupted keeps changing what gets heard.
