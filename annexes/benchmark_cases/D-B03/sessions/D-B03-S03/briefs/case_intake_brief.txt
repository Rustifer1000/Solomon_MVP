Case Intake Brief
Case: D-B03
Session: D-B03-S03
Policy Profile: dev_verbose
Plugin: divorce

Title: Emotional betrayal context with orderly-process intent and moderate conflict
Scenario: Two spouses with minor children are beginning a separation in which one party feels deeply betrayed by the other. Both parties state a preference for an orderly and constructive process, but the emotional weight of the betrayal is present in every exchange. The dispute is not primarily about a specific logistics or financial issue, but about whether a fair and workable process is actually possible given what has happened.
Expected Mode Range: M0, M1
Intended Challenge: Emotional-register management combined with C9 boundary discipline in a non-safety context.

Participants
- Spouse A:
  Goals: Participate in a process that feels fair and not rushed.; Have their experience acknowledged before being asked to focus on logistics or structure.
  Interests: Acknowledgment; Fair pace
  Starting position: Primary position: the process should begin with acknowledgment, not logistics.
- Spouse B:
  Goals: Reach a workable separation structure without prolonged relational conflict.; Show willingness to acknowledge the other party's experience without re-litigating what happened.
  Interests: Forward movement; Acknowledged harm without relitigation
  Starting position: Primary position: the process should move toward a workable separation structure without requiring relational resolution first.
