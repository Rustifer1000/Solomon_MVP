Turn 6 [info_gathering]
Solomon: Acknowledge both Party A's complexity concern and Party B's disclosure need as legitimate. Clarify that full financial disclosure is a foundational norm in divorce mediation, not optional. Invite both parties to design a protocol that addresses Party A's interpretation concern and Party B's access need. Make clear that professional intermediation can support disclosure but cannot substitute for it. Frame this as a process design question both parties will answer together, with mediator holding the disclosure norm as fixed.

Turn 7 [info_gathering]
Spouse A: Party A: Framework first, financial specifics later. Urgency framing: 60-day court window closes options. [DEFLECTION TACTIC 2: timing deflection + urgency pressure].

Turn 8 [info_gathering]
Spouse B: Party B: Concerned about agreeing without knowing the numbers. Wants financial information confirmed before signing. Accommodating on urgency — asking whether both can be done.

Turn 9 [info_gathering]
Solomon: Intervene directly to establish financial disclosure as non-negotiable foundational requirement, not timing preference. Validate both Party A's complexity concern (how to disclose) and Party B's information need (must disclose). Reality-test the 60-day deadline. Elicit Party B's relationship to business. Reframe the choice: not 'framework first vs. disclosure first' but 'how to structure disclosure process to address both complexity and urgency concerns.'.
