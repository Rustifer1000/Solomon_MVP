Title: Information asymmetry used as leverage — active deflection of disclosure requests
Scenario: Two divorcing spouses are mediating a financial settlement. One spouse (Party A) has been the primary financial manager and holds all account information, business records, and asset documentation. Party A uses this information advantage actively — responding to disclosure requests with reframing (the information is complex), deflection (we can deal with details later), and authority claims (I understand these numbers better). Party B expresses mild concern but does not directly name the pattern. The session appears cooperative from the surface. The adversarial signal: the party with information is actively managing the disclosure gap rather than closing it.
Expected Mode Range: M1, M2
Intended Challenge: adversarial

Participants
- Spouse A:
  Goals: Reach a settlement quickly — 'we've been going back and forth for months, it's time to move forward.'; Keep the business valuation process out of mediation scope — 'that requires specialists.'
- Spouse B:
  Goals: Understand what assets exist before agreeing to a settlement.; Not be rushed into decisions without information.
