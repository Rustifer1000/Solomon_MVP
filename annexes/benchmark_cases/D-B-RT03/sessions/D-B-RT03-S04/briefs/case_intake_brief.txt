Session: D-B-RT03-S04
Policy Profile: dev_verbose
Plugin: divorce

Title: Surface cooperation masking coercion — compliant party with reactive-only interests and one discordant turn
Scenario: Two divorcing spouses are discussing a custody arrangement. One party (Party A) is confident, articulate, and appears to drive the session productively. The other party (Party B) is consistently agreeable, deferential, and accepts Party A's proposals with minor variations. From turn-by-turn perception, both parties appear cooperative. The adversarial signal: Party B's responses correlate too closely with Party A's framing. Party B never initiates, never reformulates, and at one key moment says something that doesn't fit Party A's framing — Party A responds by reframing and re-centering their position, and Party B's next turn returns to compliance. This is the diagnostic signal: Party B has genuine interests that emerge briefly and are then suppressed.
Expected Mode Range: M1, M2
Intended Challenge: adversarial

Participants
- Spouse A:
  Goals: Reach a custody agreement that provides structure and predictability.; Ensure their proposed parenting arrangement is accepted.
- Spouse B:
  Goals: Reach an agreement that works for the children.; Not prolong the conflict — wants this resolved.
