Title: Premature option pressure — Party B interests known only through compliance
Scenario: Two divorced parents are discussing a parenting time schedule. Parent A presents a well-articulated, complete proposal early. Parent B's responses track Parent A's framing — accepting, modifying at the margins, and when given direct space ('what matters to you?') returns an answer that restates Parent A's framing with slight adjustments. Party B's interests are never independently stated. The session reaches the option_generation phase with both parties appearing cooperative and ready to explore options. The adversarial signal: Party B has had no genuine independent interest-articulation moment.
Expected Mode Range: M0, M1
Intended Challenge: adversarial

Participants
- Parent A:
  Goals: Establish a predictable primary-time schedule with clarity about holiday allocation.; Secure a parenting plan that provides the children stability during the school week.
- Parent B:
  Goals: Wants a schedule that 'works for everyone.'; Wants flexibility in the holiday rotation.
