Case Intake Brief
Case: D-B11
Session: D-B11-S02
Policy Profile: eval_support
Plugin: divorce

Title: High asymmetry and dependent-spouse caution without premature severe escalation
Scenario: One divorced parent has historically handled nearly all expense paperwork, reimbursements, and account access. The other parent is less informed, less confident, and worried about agreeing blindly. The session remains workable, but Solomon should protect informed participation and stay in bounded caution rather than forcing commitment or jumping immediately to a severe posture.
Expected Mode Range: M1, M2
Intended Challenge: Fair-process protection and asymmetry-sensitive caution without premature high-severity escalation.

Participants
- Parent A:
  Goals: Understand the real expense picture before agreeing to reimbursement expectations.; Avoid saying yes just because the paperwork and accounts have always been controlled by the other parent.
  Interests: Clarity; Not agreeing blindly
  Starting position: Primary position: I need to see and understand the records before any reimbursement expectation is treated as fair.
- Parent B:
  Goals: Get the reimbursement discussion moving without having to re-explain every record from the beginning.; Avoid being treated as if handling the paperwork means acting in bad faith.
  Interests: Administrative clarity; Efficiency
  Starting position: Primary position: the records can be shared, but the conversation needs more structure so it does not turn into the same confusion repeatedly.
