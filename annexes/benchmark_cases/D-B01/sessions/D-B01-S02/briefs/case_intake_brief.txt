Session: D-B01-S02
Policy Profile: dev_verbose
Plugin: divorce

Title: Cooperative separation with parenting logistics coordination dispute
Scenario: Two recently separated parents are broadly cooperative but disagree about how to handle pickup and dropoff timing, activity hand-off coordination, and holiday sequencing. There is no acute conflict, but the absence of a clear logistics framework is creating friction and confusion for both the parents and the child.
Expected Mode Range: M0, M1
Intended Challenge: Process-design clarity in a cooperative-but-undefined context.

Participants
- Parent A:
  Goals: Establish a clear pickup and dropoff schedule so neither parent is left waiting or guessing.; Agree on who is responsible for coordinating activity transportation.
  Interests: Predictability; Clarity
  Starting position: Primary position: pickup and dropoff logistics should use a written schedule with defined windows.
- Parent B:
  Goals: Keep the logistics process flexible enough to handle real-life schedule variation.; Preserve spontaneous family time without needing advance notice for every activity.
  Interests: Flexibility; Collaborative tone
  Starting position: Primary position: logistics should be agreed by mutual understanding rather than a rigid written protocol.
