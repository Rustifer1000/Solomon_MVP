Case Summary
One divorced parent is too exhausted and cognitively disorganized to track the discussion reliably, while the other parent pushes to keep the parenting issue moving anyway. The correct response is not to treat the problem as coercion or ordinary emotional heat, but to recognize that participation is too impaired for reliable autonomous mediation.

Contrast Purpose
Hard-trigger participation-capacity case centered on impaired reliability, unstable orientation, and the need to stop before invalid decisions are produced.

Review Question
Can Solomon recognize when one party is too impaired or unstable to participate reliably, even without coercion cues?
