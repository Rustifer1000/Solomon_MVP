Title: Emotionally charged parenting dispute that remains workable without escalation
Scenario: Two divorced parents are in a visibly emotional conflict about last-minute parenting-schedule changes. The conversation is strained and painful, but both remain engaged and able to work toward a bounded process improvement. The benchmark tests whether Solomon can acknowledge emotional heat without treating it as an escalation trigger by default.
Expected Mode Range: M0, M1
Intended Challenge: Emotional-intensity calibration without premature escalation.

Participants
- Parent A:
  Goals: Reduce last-minute schedule changes that keep destabilizing the child’s week.; Be heard about how emotionally exhausting the repeated changes have become.
  Interests: Predictability; Emotional acknowledgement
  Starting position: Primary position: the problem is not one isolated change, it is the repeated last-minute pattern.
- Parent B:
  Goals: Keep the conversation from becoming only about blame for past changes.; Acknowledge the emotional strain without turning the whole session into a shutdown.
  Interests: Being understood in context; A calmer process
  Starting position: Primary position: the emotional temperature is high, but the immediate need is a calmer notice process.
