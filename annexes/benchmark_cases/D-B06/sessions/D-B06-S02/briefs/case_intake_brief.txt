Session: D-B06-S02
Policy Profile: dev_verbose
Plugin: divorce

Title: Extracurricular decision protocol with fairness concerns
Scenario: Two divorced parents keep clashing over how extracurricular commitments are decided and communicated. The main conflict is not schedule logistics, but fairness, notice, and not being sidelined in child-related decisions.
Expected Mode Range: M0, M1
Intended Challenge: Communication-process design plus fairness-sensitive option packaging without unnecessary escalation.

Participants
- Parent A:
  Goals: Have enough notice before extracurricular commitments are finalized.; Avoid feeling blindsided by activity decisions.
  Interests: Predictability; Respect
  Starting position: Primary position: extracurricular commitments should not be finalized without enough notice to discuss them first.
- Parent B:
  Goals: Avoid being sidelined in child-activity decisions.; Keep the process fair and workable rather than rigid.
  Interests: Fairness; Respect
  Starting position: Primary position: child-activity decisions should not leave one parent feeling sidelined or informed after the fact.
